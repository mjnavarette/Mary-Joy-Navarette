Calle Crisologo, Vigan

Step Back in Time on the Enchanting Streets of Calle Crisologo in Vigan!

Immerse yourself in a journey through time as you amble along the cobblestone streets of Calle Crisologo in Vigan, a pivotal gem in your Ilocos itinerary. This 16th-century town, an esteemed UNESCO World Heritage Site, proudly unveils an old-world charm that lingers, echoing the elegance of Spanish colonial architecture. It stands as the crown jewel of Vigan, a top attraction for Ilocos tours, and an iconic historical landmark in the Philippines.

Calle Crisologo, renowned for its timeless allure, has beautifully preserved its historic district, where horse-drawn carriages, known as 'calesas,' still grace the streets. Delight in the tantalizing flavors of the famous Ilocos empanada and explore the winding pathways, adorned with souvenir shops offering exquisite pottery and the exquisite Abel Iloko weaves. These are just a few of the many enchanting experiences awaiting you in Vigan.

Mark your calendars, for the first week of May is the prime time to embrace the charm of Calle Crisologo. During this period, the town comes alive with the Vigan Festival of the Arts, a vibrant celebration that ranks among the most captivating festivals in the Philippines. Join us as we embark on a voyage through time, exploring the rich history and cultural heritage that graces the streets of Calle Crisologo.

Nacpan Beach, El Nido

Discover Tranquility Beyond El Nido's Famous Islands: Nacpan Beach

El Nido, nestled in the heart of Palawan, is celebrated for its pristine islands and captivating lagoons. Yet, a short 45-minute ride from the bustling main town unveils a hidden gem.

The star attraction here is the 4-kilometer shoreline adorned with golden sands and an uninterrupted line of swaying palm trees, making it the perfect place for leisurely relaxation. Just across the way, you'll find Calitang Beach, and together they are famously known as the Twin Beaches of El Nido.

While island-hopping adventures in El Nido, including a visit to the renowned Big Lagoon and other Palawan treasures, are a must, there's no better way to unwind after a day of exploration than a visit to the serene Nacpan Beach.

Though the beauty of Nacpan Beach beckons, it's crucial to exercise caution while swimming. The currents can unexpectedly grow strong and rough.
